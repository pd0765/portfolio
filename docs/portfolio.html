Featured Project · Financial Econometrics

CAPM & Black-Litterman Implied Returns: An Empirical Demonstration

> 0.997 Absolute correlation — every month
198 Monthly snapshots
16+ yrs S&P 500 data (2006–2022)

Demonstrates, using over sixteen years of real-world S&P 500 data, that the expected returns implied by CAPM and those implied by the Black-Litterman model (when no investor views are imposed) correlate at greater than 0.997 in absolute value — not as a numerical coincidence, but as a direct consequence of both models sharing the same equilibrium foundation. The finding is validated across 198 consecutive monthly snapshots, not a single time period.

Cross-sectional correlation exceeds 0.997 in absolute value in every one of the 198 months sampled. Sign inversions in 25 months are explained as a mechanical artifact of negative market excess returns, not a substantive disagreement between models.

LLM Evaluation · Quantitative Finance

LLM Numeric Reasoning in Financial Calculations

Controlled experiment benchmarking Claude across six conditions — varying platform, tool access, and system prompt — on multi-step financial metric computation (Sharpe, Sortino, Maximum Drawdown) across 20 ETFs spanning 2019–2023. Core finding: tool access is the only variable that matters. Without code execution, the model produces confident, well-formatted output that is wrong 97% of the time. RAG · LLM Evaluation · Financial NLP

AI Hallucination Detection in Corporate Earnings Analysis

RAG pipeline measuring LLM hallucination rates when answering financial questions drawn from SEC 10-K filings across 8 S&P 500 companies and 4 fiscal years (FY2021–FY2024). A confidence-gating mitigation (cosine similarity threshold of 0.65) reduces overall hallucination from 33% to 8% — a 75% reduction — while preserving 95% of answerable accuracy. Residual failures and persistent weak spots are documented in the context of SR 11-7 model risk management.
